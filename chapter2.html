The deep dive

By: Alex Zerbach

Now what do I do?

My stomach lurched as I pressed entered. Before that night I had never entered the "deep web". I had always heard about it. Truth be told, I, like many novice computer users, had no idea how to access a part of the internet that wasn't accessible through a Google search. But after reading a news story on CNN about the "deep web" I knew I had to try and find it. What I wasn't expecting is what came next. To say it ruined my life is an understatement. But to say that those 3 weeks of my life weren't the 3 best weeks of my life would also be an understatement.

I don't even remember the name of the website I found to learn how to get on the "deep web". But I remember feeling nervous when I was one keystroke away from accessing a part of the internet that not even the FBI could contain. My biggest fear was that my accounts would get hacked and somehow my bank accounts would be drained.

So there I sat, glued to my computer screen in my tiny studio apartment. My buddy Josh had just called and asked if I wanted to go out for a round of drinks. I blew him off, this was much more interesting. I do one last Google search for how to protect your passwords from being hacked and I hit enter. What happened next blew my mind. It worked. I was in. Now what the hell do I do?
